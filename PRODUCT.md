# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Speedcubers practicing on desktop and mobile, typically during drills, recognition training, or quick review between solves. They need to identify a case fast, confirm the starting state visually, and recall the algorithm without wading through clutter.

## Product Purpose
This product is a compact cube cheat sheet for practice sessions. It organizes cases by method and group, shows the starting cube state, and presents notation clearly so users can move from recognition to execution with minimal friction. Success means the sheet feels immediate, legible, and dependable during active practice.

## Brand Personality
Technical, focused, calm. The interface should feel precise and efficient, with enough visual structure to support recognition, but without noise that competes with the algorithms.

## Anti-references
Avoid cluttered trainer apps, neon gamer UI, and heavy dashboard aesthetics. It should not feel over-instrumented, flashy, or crowded with decorative controls.

## Design Principles
1. Recognition first: case state and algorithm should be scannable in seconds.
2. Practice without friction: reduce visual noise and keep interactions minimal.
3. Calm precision: present technical information clearly without feeling sterile.
4. Mobile-ready utility: maintain legibility and touch comfort on smaller screens.
5. Visual meaning over ornament: every visual element should support recognition or recall.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target good color contrast consistent with WCAG AA for core text and controls. Maintain large enough touch targets for mobile use. Preserve clarity for users practicing under time pressure or in varied lighting conditions.
